Welcome to our Celebration

The School of Computer Science in the University of Hull is in its fiftieth year. So we thought we'd have a bit of a do.

It's for any of our past or present students or staff who want to come along to relive a bit of their past and take a look into our future. There's quite a bit going on and we'd love to have you along for the ride.

It all happens over the weekend of 12th October 2024

We start with an evening welcome event on Friday the 11th leading into our big celebration day on Saturday 12th. We'll have robot rugby for the kids, research talks, Hull Stories, Retro video games, 3D printing, and a 'Computer Science Welcome Party Redux' where you can relive your first social engagement in Hull, complete with a dodgy quiz.

If you fancy a doing a bit of coding while you are here we'll also be running a Three Thing Game hackathon. Get your three 'game things' at our online event on the evening of Wednesday 9th October and then spend as much time as you like building something impressive in our labs.

Lightning Talks

If you want to give a lightning talk during the celebration we would love to see you strutt your stuff. Your talk can be as long or as short as you like. Although more than six words and shorter than three hours would be preferrable. If you are stuck for topics we will be giving prizes for the best talk in these categories:

- The most important thing that I learned at Hull
- The most important "Computer Science" thing I learned at Hull
- The least important "Computer Science" thing I learned at Hull
- What I wish I"d known when I arrived
- The daftest thing I did at Hull
- The best thing I did at Hull

If you have an idea for a category of your own, let us know on Discord.
